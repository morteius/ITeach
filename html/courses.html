PHYSICS FOR COMPUTING

This course teaches students the physical fundamentals behind computer hardware and technology design.

It explores key concepts such as transistor technology, electronic components, free electron theories in solids, semiconductors and their devices, basic logic circuits, and integrated circuits. The course emphasizes how physics, mathematics, and logic have contributed to the creation of powerful computing machines, enabling students to appreciate the innovations shaping their field and make informed decisions regarding the tools and materials essential to their professional practice.

COMPUTER PROGRAMMING

This course introduces to the fundamentals of programming using the C programming language.

The course focuses on building a strong foundation by exploring basic programming concepts, including program structure, syntax, variables, data types, operators, and control flow mechanisms. It provides hands-on experience with programming environments, debugging, and problem-solving through structured programming approaches. Students learn essential C programming constructs such as decision-making, loops, arrays, strings, pointers, and memory management while emphasizing proper coding practices, logical reasoning, and coding discipline.

MATHEMATICS IN THE MODERN WORLD

This course explores the nature of mathematics, highlighting its practical, intellectual, and aesthetic dimensions.

It introduces mathematics as an exploration of patterns in nature and an application of inductive and deductive reasoning, encouraging appreciation beyond formulas to its logic, reasoning, and beauty. The course emphasizes mathematics as a tool for managing personal finances, making social choices, understanding geometric designs, securing data, and fairly dividing resources. Through diverse exercises, students engage with mathematics as a way of knowing and apply it to real-life scenarios, enhancing their understanding and skills.

HUMAN REPRODUCTION

This lecture course examines the biological, cultural, and ecological aspects of human reproduction.

It covers reproductive anatomy and physiology, stages of development, reproductive health, and the impact of environmental and societal factors. The course delves into contemporary issues in reproductive biotechnology, ethical considerations, and common misconceptions, fostering a deeper understanding of human reproduction. Through lectures, student-led discussions, and research analysis, students gain insights into reproductive health, challenges, and advancements while emphasizing informed decision-making and care practices.
